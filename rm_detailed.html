Proposed Research Methodology

1. Research Approach

The study uses a mixed-method approach combining:

  • Quantitative Analysis: Evaluate performance with metrics like accuracy, precision.
  • Qualitative Insights: Understand ethical impacts of content moderation.

2. Data Collection

Datasets for training include:

  • Jigsaw Toxic Dataset: Labeled toxic content (e.g., obscene, threat).
  • Custom AI Responses: GPT-generated content labeled as harmful, neutral, or safe.

3. Preprocessing

Steps to prepare data:

  • Cleaning: Remove symbols, normalize text, handle missing data.
  • Annotation: Label text as harmful, contextually harmful, or safe.
  • Tokenization: Use transformer-compatible tokenizers and word embeddings.

4. Model Development

Steps for building the model:

  • Architecture: Combine transformers with explainability techniques.
  • Training: Fine-tune pre-trained models with labeled data.
  • Objectives: Detect harmful content, classify categories, explain flagged content.

5. Evaluation

Evaluate the model with:

  • Metrics: Accuracy, precision, recall, F1 score, and explainability metrics.
  • Comparative Analysis: Compare with existing systems like OpenAI Moderation API.
